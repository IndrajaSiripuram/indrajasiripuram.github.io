Streamlining the Hiring Experience with AI-Powered Job Ad Creation

As part of Walmart’s ongoing efforts to enhance its recruitment process, I collaborated with a cross-functional team to improve the hiring manager experience through AI-powered solutions. The project aimed to simplify the creation of job ads and interview guides while ensuring consistency, efficiency, and higher candidate quality.

Project type:End-to-end AI-Powered Job Ad Creation mobile and web application

Role:UX/UI designer

Tools:Figma, Figjam, Mural Board

Duration:Q3 2024

2. Research & Journey Mapping

3. Design

4. Iteration

Project Background

Problem Statement:

Hiring managers faced significant challenges creating comprehensive and engaging job ads, often resulting in inconsistent postings and poorly targeted candidates. Additionally, interview guides lacked specificity and standardization, further complicating the process. These inefficiencies led to extended hiring timelines, reduced candidate quality, and lower hiring manager satisfaction scores.

Purpose:

To address these pain points, we developed the Augmented Job Advert (AJA) service. This AI-powered tool aims to accelerate job ad and interview guide creation, reduce manual efforts, and ensure alignment with business requirements.

Objective:

To improve the speed, efficiency, and quality of job ads and interview guides, reducing time-to-fill and enhancing the overall recruitment experience for both hiring managers and candidates.

Hypothesis

By empowering hiring managers with the AJA service, we anticipated:

Significant time savings in creating and refining job ads and interview guides.

Improved quality and standardization of job ads, leading to better hiring outcomes.

Enhanced hiring manager satisfaction through a streamlined and user-friendly process..

Process and Approach

1.Personas & Populations:

Initial deployment targeted Walmart Home Office U.S. populations, including hiring managers, people partners, and recruiters. Future iterations aimed to expand to field hiring managers at Walmart and Sam’s Club.

2.Workflow Redesign:

We revamped the end-to-end workflow for job ad creation, incorporating:

  • AI-driven suggestions for job responsibilities and qualifications.
  • Automatic standardization of interview guides tailored to specific roles.

    • 3.Collaborative Development:

    The project adhered to a “Four-in-the-Box” (4itB) approach, with dedicated leads from product, business, engineering, and UX collaborating to ensure seamless execution.

    4.Interview Guide Enhancements:

    To address inconsistencies in interview preparation, we introduced templates that align interview questions with role-specific competencies. This not only saved time but also improved the quality of interviews.

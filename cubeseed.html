OVERVIEW

Cubeseed is a contract-driven virtual marketplace that facilitates agro-trade and boosts agricultural production in Africa by empowering farmers and providing investment security. Through our web and mobile applications, we deliver value to the agricultural ecosystem by utilizing technology to ensure security, transparency, and connectivity.
Our goal is to connect the agricultural ecosystem and stimulate specialization in the value chain while ensuring investment security to attract and sustain investments.

The Problem

Over 180 million smallholder farmers make up 90% of the farming population and are responsible for 80% of the food produced. However, they are plagued by a lack of access to credit, quality inputs, and markets. These challenges have a direct impact on farmers’ income, food security, food quality, and food affordability.

The Solution

Cubessed provides a platform for the farmers to find the investors and gain maximum profit with the 100% transparency also helps Investors by providing all the destails and inputs with the best investing options

PAPER WIREFRAMES

The main goal of the website was to make it easy for the users to find a pet and also make the donation process easy. We also wanted the website to be simple and easy to navigate.

The wireframes were designed by sketching some quick alternatives to each screen and the best options were marked and are put together in a single screen and formed a wireframe.

LOW-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

LO-Fi prototypes are created for different screens to make the overall experience of the users without any confusion. The designs are slightly modified with respect to the screen sizes. The continuity is maintained through out the website for different screen sizes.

  1. Option to redirect easily and have access to adoption process.
  2. Filters will help users in saving their time.
  3. Users can view the deatils about the pet and vaccination history.

USABILITY STUDY FINDINGS

We conducted two rounds of unmoderated usability with 5 participants. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups and the second study used the hi fidelity prototypes and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

1. Search is moved to the nav bar to have access through out the website.

2. Pets are categorized for easy access.

3. Filters are moved to the corner and fixed position on scroll.

4. View button is removed and a icon is added.

5. Share icon is added to make it easy for the users to share.

Interaction design

Pet Adoption Process

User has multilple options to click from anywhere in the page and move to the adoption page.

The process include searching for a pet and filling a form with all the details including flexible time to pick up.

Responsive web design

Maintained consistency through out the designs and designed screens for multiple devices with mobile first approach.

Filters to save user time

making the filter tab fixed seem to be a small thing but it really made a huge differnce.

The users were much more comfortable with the fixed filter button as it is visible to them through out the screen. Instead of going to the top of the page to change the filters they can do it from any where in the page.

TAKE AWAYS

In conclusion, the design process for the ADOPt app involved conducting user research, creating personas, conducting competitive analysis, sketching and prototyping, and conducting usability testing. Through this process, I was able to identify the key pain points and desires of users, and design an app that addresses those needs. The final result is an website that makes it easy for people to adopt a pet in few easy clicks. I learned a lot throughout the process, also gained valuable insights into the needs and preferences of my target audience, and I improved my design skills and knowledge of user-centered design principles.
